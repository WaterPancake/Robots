Two Axis Cart Pole

Extending the canonical cart-pole problem with an additional axis of traversal and an extra degree of freedom on the inverted pendulum.

Environment & State Space

The inverted two-axis pendulum state vector is:

$$\mathbf{x} = \left[ x,\; y,\; \theta_x,\; \theta_y,\; \dot{x},\; \dot{y},\; \dot{\theta}_x,\; \dot{\theta}_y \right]$$
VariableDescriptionMinMax
$x$Cart position along x-axis−22
$y$Cart position along y-axis−22
$\theta_x$Pole angle along x-axis (rad)$0$$2\pi$
$\theta_y$Pole angle along y-axis (rad)$0$$2\pi$

Control Inputs

The system has two control inputs corresponding to linear forces applied to the cart:

$$u = \begin{bmatrix} F_x \\ F_y \end{bmatrix}$$

Reward Functions

Basic Swing-Up

$$\mathcal{R}_1(s,a) = 2 + \cos(\theta_x) + \cos(\theta_y)$$

Centering & Swing-Up

Adds a penalty proportional to distance from the origin to encourage both swing-up and centering:

$$\mathcal{R}_2(s,a) = 4\bigl(\cos(\theta_x) + \cos(\theta_y)\bigr) - \bigl(x^2 + y^2\bigr)$$

The coefficient of 4 offsets the range mismatch between the pole-balancing reward $[-2,2]$ and the distance penalty $[0,-4]$.

Assumptions: no damping on force applied to the cart and no friction on the cart.

Kinematics

The pole tip lives in $\mathbb{R}^3$ while the cart moves in $\mathbb{R}^2$.

Position

$$\mathbf{x}_p = \begin{bmatrix} x_c + \ell\sin(\theta_x) \\ y_c + \ell\sin(\theta_y) \\ \ell\cos(\theta_x)\cos(\theta_y) \end{bmatrix}$$

Velocity

$$\dot{\mathbf{x}}_p = \begin{bmatrix} \dot x_c + \ell\cos(\theta_x)\,\dot\theta_x \\ \dot y_c + \ell\cos(\theta_y)\,\dot\theta_y \\ -\ell\sin(\theta_x)\cos(\theta_y)\,\dot\theta_x - \ell\sin(\theta_y)\cos(\theta_x)\,\dot\theta_y \end{bmatrix}$$

Equations of Motion

Derived via the Lagrangian $L = T - V$, where $T$ and $V$ are kinetic and potential energy.

Kinetic Energy

Pole (translational)

$$T_p = \tfrac{1}{2}\,m_p\!\left(\dot x_p^2 + \dot y_p^2 + \dot z_p^2\right)$$

Cart

$$T_c = \tfrac{1}{2}\,m_c\!\left(\dot x_c^2 + \dot y_c^2\right)$$

Pole (rotational)

$$T_r = \tfrac{1}{2}\,I\!\left(\dot\theta_x^2 + \dot\theta_y^2\right), \qquad I = m_p\ell^2$$
$$T = T_p + T_c + T_r$$

Potential Energy

$$V = m_p\,g\,\ell\,\cos(\theta_x)\cos(\theta_y)$$

Euler–Lagrange Equation

Using generalized coordinates $q = \begin{bmatrix} x_c & y_c & \theta_x & \theta_y \end{bmatrix}^T$:

$$\frac{d}{dt}\!\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot q_i}\right) - \frac{\partial L}{\partial q_i} = Q_i$$

Dynamics Equations

Cart: $x_c$

$$F_x = \ddot x_c(m_c + m_p) + m_p\ell\!\left(\cos(\theta_x)\,\ddot\theta_x - \sin(\theta_x)\,\dot\theta_x^2\right)$$

Pole: $\theta_x$

Generalized momentum (small-angle approx.)

$$\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot\theta_x} \approx m_p\ell\cos(\theta_x)\,\dot x_c + \dot\theta_x\!\left[m_p\ell^2 + I\right]$$

Time derivative

$$\frac{d}{dt}\!\left(\frac{\partial L}{\partial \dot\theta_x}\right) \approx m_p\ell\cos(\theta_x)\,\ddot x_c - m_p\ell\sin(\theta_x)\,\dot\theta_x\,\dot x_c + \ddot\theta_x\!\left[m_p\ell^2 + I\right]$$

Resulting force equation

$$F_{\theta_x} = m_p\ell\cos(\theta_x)\,\ddot x_c + \ddot\theta_x\!\left[m_p\ell^2 + I\right] + m_p\ell^2\sin(\theta_x)\cos(\theta_y)\,\dot\theta_x^2 + m_p g\ell\sin(\theta_x)\cos(\theta_y)$$
N.B.: There is an open question on whether the gravitational term should be subtracted rather than added. Further derivation needed to confirm the sign.

Linearization

To design an LQR controller we linearize about the upright fixed point using: $\cos\theta \approx 1$, $\sin\theta \approx \theta$, $\theta^2 \approx 0$.

Coupled linear equations

$$\ddot x_c(m_c + m_p) + m_p\ell\,\ddot\theta_x = F_x$$ $$m_p\ell\,\ddot x_c + \ddot\theta_x(I + m_p\ell^2) + m_p g\ell\,\theta_x = 0$$

Matrix form

$$\underbrace{\begin{bmatrix} m_c + m_p & m_p\ell \\ m_p\ell & I + m_p\ell^2 \end{bmatrix}}_M \begin{bmatrix} \ddot x_c \\ \ddot\theta_x \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix} F_x \\ -m_p g\ell\,\theta_x \end{bmatrix}$$

Solving via $M^{-1}$

With $D = \det(M)$:

$$\ddot x_c = F_x\,\frac{m_c + m_p}{D} \;-\; \theta_x\,\frac{m_p^2\ell^2 g}{D}$$ $$\ddot\theta_x = F_x\,\frac{m_p\ell}{D} \;-\; \theta_x\,\frac{I\,m_p g\ell \;-\; m_p^2\ell^3 g}{D}$$

By symmetry, the $y$-axis equations are identical with $x \to y$:

$$\ddot y_c = F_y\,\frac{m_c + m_p}{D} - \theta_y\,\frac{m_p^2\ell^2 g}{D}$$ $$\ddot\theta_y = F_y\,\frac{m_p\ell}{D} - \theta_y\,\frac{I\,m_p g\ell - m_p^2\ell^3 g}{D}$$

Cost-To-Go & LQR

$$J = \int_0^\infty \!\left[\mathbf{x}(t)^T Q\,\mathbf{x}(t) + \mathbf{u}(t)^T R\,\mathbf{u}(t)\right] dt$$

Weight matrices

$$Q = \operatorname{diag}\!\left(10,\; 10,\; 100,\; 100,\; 1,\; 1,\; 1,\; 1\right)$$ $$R = \begin{bmatrix} 1 \\ 1 \end{bmatrix}$$

Emphasis is placed on controlling $\theta_x$ and $\theta_y$ (weight 100) while still penalizing cart displacement (weight 10).

Using the linear approximation $\dot{\mathbf{x}} = A\mathbf{x} + B\mathbf{u}$, the optimal gain matrix $K$ is obtained by solving the Continuous Algebraic Riccati Equation via scipy.linalg.solve_continuous_are(A, B, Q, R). The control law is then $\mathbf{u} = -K\mathbf{x}$.
